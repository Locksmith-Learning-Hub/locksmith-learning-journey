# 🧠 Module 1: Lock Fundamentals

## 📌 Overview
This module covers the foundational concepts of locksmithing, focusing on understanding different types of locks, their internal components, and how locking mechanisms function. A clear grasp of these basics is essential before moving into practical and advanced techniques.

---

## 🔐 Types of Locks

### 1. Cylinder Locks (Euro & Mortice)
- **Euro Cylinder Locks**
  - Commonly used in uPVC and modern doors
  - Easily replaceable without removing the entire lock mechanism
  - Operated using a key that aligns internal pins

- **Mortice Locks**
  - Installed inside the door body
  - Typically used in wooden doors
  - Provide higher security with deeper fitting into the door

---

### 2. Deadbolt Locks
- Provide strong security through a solid metal bolt
- Operated by key or thumb turn
- Resistant to forced entry compared to spring latch locks
- Common in residential properties

---

### 3. Padlocks
- Portable and external locks
- Used for gates, storage units, and temporary security
- Available in different security levels (basic to high-security)

---

## ⚙️ Basic Lock Anatomy

Understanding internal components is key to grasping how locks function.

### Key Components:
- **Pins**
  - Small metal components inside the cylinder
  - Must align correctly for the lock to open

- **Springs**
  - Push pins into position
  - Maintain tension inside the lock mechanism

- **Plug (Cylinder Core)**
  - Rotating part where the key is inserted
  - Turns when pins align correctly

- **Shear Line**
  - The line where the plug meets the outer housing
  - When pins align at this line → lock opens

---

## 🔄 How Locking Mechanisms Work

1. Key is inserted into the lock  
2. Key cuts (shape) push pins to specific heights  
3. Pins align at the shear line  
4. Plug becomes free to rotate  
5. Lock opens  

### ❗ If pins do not align:
- Plug cannot rotate  
- Lock remains closed
